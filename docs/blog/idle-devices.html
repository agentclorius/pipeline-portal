The €1,000 Devices Collecting Dust in Your Drawers

Why redeploying idle IT assets beats buying new — and how to do it at scale

Every enterprise has them. Laptops in desk drawers. Phones in storage closets. Devices sitting with former employees who left months ago. IT assets that still work, still have value, but aren't doing anything.

These aren't broken devices. They're not obsolete. They're just... idle.

And while they sit there, your company is ordering brand new hardware at €1,000+ per unit — if you can get it at all.

The hidden fleet nobody talks about

The numbers are staggering. According to research by Oomnitza and YouGov:

  • 50% of companies have lost at least 5% of their endpoint devices when employees leave
  • Among large enterprises (10,000+ employees), 25% lose more than 10% of devices at offboarding
  • Most of these devices still contain corporate data

The combination of remote work, hybrid offices, and high employee turnover has created a massive pool of untracked, underutilised IT assets scattered across homes and offices worldwide.

Why this matters more in 2026

If this math wasn't compelling enough, the market is making it urgent:

Dell and Lenovo have announced price increases of up to 15% — and that's just the beginning. The Consumer Technology Association projects 20-30% increases if tariffs escalate further.

  • Memory shortages are crushing supply — HBM production for AI is eating consumer DRAM capacity
  • Sub-€600 business laptops are becoming nearly impossible to source
  • Lead times are stretching — 12+ weeks for some chipsets, 40+ weeks for advanced components
  • Some vendors are selling PCs without RAM — customers must source their own
